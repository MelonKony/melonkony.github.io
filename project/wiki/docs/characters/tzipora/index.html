    Tzipora Desmoisnes #

    Introduction #

    Tzipora Desmoisnes (also Zelda lo Ula Desmoisnes, born May 5 2047) is a Vekllei Gregori Baby and librarian, and is the character utopia of the [Petticoat Project]. Born to artist and political activist Mette Sumoisnesn and an unknown father, the family moved to Colombia when Tzipora was six months old. Her return to Vekllei as a teen-ager would mark her integration into Vekllei society and her diagnosis as Vekllei’s first victim of Gregori-Heitzfeld syndrome.

    Tzipora is characterised by her subdued disposition and conservative tastes, informed in part by her youth growing up poor in [[Colombia]] and the [[United States]]. Although she is naturally quiet and independent, she loves dearly her adoptive father Baron Desmoisnes and family friend Ayn Rumouisen, with whom she is very affectionate. Tzipora struggles (but remains undiagnosed) with post-traumatic stress disorder, which interferes with her sleep and manifests as several triggers, including large dogs and lying on her chest. In addition, she is recognisable for her many quirks and obsessions, most obviously nervous pulling and rubbing of her clothes and nose. She is relatively unknown at a national level in Vekllei, outside of an occasional medical curiosity, but is relatively famous internationally as the oldest surviving [[Gregori Baby]].

    Early Life #

    After the American invasion of Colombia in 2057, Mette paid an education broker several thousands of pesos to arrange Tzipora’s education in California. Although Tzipora would graduate in 2061, she had lost contact with her mother and had lapsed on her education visa. Without an allowance and barred from legitimate work, she moved to Utah the same year in search of income.

    After finding work as a motel maid and residence at a women’s shelter, Tzipora was witness to much of the industrial unrest in Utah in the early 2060s. After being threatened and later nearly murdered by strikebreakers for her association with the United Mine Workers, at that time an illegal organisation, she met [[Baron Desmoisnes]], an agent of the Vekllei Americas Bureau. In 2063, she would flee Utah under his advice and return to Vekllei under emergency asylum.

    Since arriving in [[Vekllei]] at the age of 16, Tzipora has received two doctorates from Montre University and currently works as a community librarian in the small Montre village of Montre-Lola.
